Geographic Exploration — Notebook 2 Notebook 2 is a focused, geography-only exploration of customer_info's latitude/longitude fields, built to answer one question before modelling starts: is there a meaningful spatial structure in the customer base that the segmentation should be aware of (or deliberately exclude)? It produces a static scatter of customer locations, an interactive Mapbox/Plotly view, a Folium map with marker clustering, a log-scaled hexbin density map, and then a focused “hotspot” analysis — it programmatically finds the highest-density grid cell, measures its distance to recognisable landmarks, and statistically compares the customers who live inside that hotspot with those who live outside it on variables such as age, education, household size, promotion usage, total spend, product diversity, store visits, complaints and tenure. Note for the reader of this report: at the time of inspection, this notebook's cells had not been (re-)executed — its outputs are empty, so no rendered charts could be extracted for inclusion here. This does not affect the final segmentation, because the project deliberately keeps latitude and longitude out of the clustering distance (a decision explained and re-confirmed in Notebook 3, Section 9.4, and in Notebook 4, Section 10): including raw geolocation in a distance-based model risks creating geographic clusters instead of behavioural ones, which would defeat the purpose of the exercise. Geography is therefore reserved purely for profiling — a “does any segment happen to cluster in space?” sanity check performed after the segments already exist. The one practical recommendation here is to re-run Notebook 2 before submission so that its hotspot findings (e.g., whether the dense Lisbon-area cluster differs systematically in age, spend or loyalty from the rest of the base) are visible as evidence in the final report, since the narrative groundwork for an interesting insight is already written — it just needs the cells executed and the resulting charts captured. 6. Customer Segmentation — Clustering Methodology and Model Selection — Notebook 3 This is the central notebook of the project (112 cells, 46 of them code) and the one that will most influence the “Technical quality of the Segmentation” mark. Rather than jumping straight to “KMeans with k = 8”, the notebook builds its case in layers: it first compares the two candidate preprocessing pipelines from Notebook 1, then compares scalers, then searches for k using three independent diagnostics (inertia, silhouette and dendrograms), then validates the chosen solution with three different 2-D projections, then stress-tests the feature set itself (the “petfood” experiment), and finally benchmarks the chosen KMeans solution against four alternative clustering families before committing.
