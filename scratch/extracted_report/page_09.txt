Data Cleaning and Feature Engineering — Notebook 1 Notebook 1 turns the audit from Notebook 0 into a concrete, reproducible cleaning and feature-engineering pipeline, and exports the datasets that feed the clustering stage. It re-loads the raw file independently (rather than depending on Notebook 0's in-memory state), which is a small but important reproducibility choice. 4.1 Cleaning: years, negatives, types and cyclical time The cleaning stage walks through, in order: (i) detecting invalid future values in year_first_transaction and customer_birthdate by anchoring everything to the current year via pd.Timestamp.now().year; (ii) scanning every numerical column for impossible negative values with .lt(0).any(); (iii) repairing the negative percentages identified in Notebook 0 (clipping percentage_of_products_bought_promotion back into [0, 1]); and (iv) converting object columns into proper datetime64 and numeric dtypes. The most elegant step here is the cyclical encoding of typical_hour: rather than leaving a 24-hour clock as a plain integer (where 23h and 1h would look “far apart” to a distance-based model even though they are only two hours apart), the notebook maps the hour onto a 2-D sine/cosine coordinate system. This is a textbook-correct treatment of cyclical features and shows real care about what a clustering distance actually measures.
