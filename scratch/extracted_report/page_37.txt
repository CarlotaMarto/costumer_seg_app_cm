This step is what brings the final exported customer count back up to 33,038 — matching the cleaned dataset exactly and, in turn, matching the row counts of customer_segments.csv and id_and_cluster.csv (33,038 data rows, confirmed in Section 9 of this report). It is a small implementation detail with an outsized effect on the credibility of the deliverable: every customer in the original dataset ends up with a cluster label, with none silently dropped — which is exactly what the brief's “a CSV with the cluster of each client” requirement calls for. 7. Cluster Characterization and Segment Profiles — Notebook 4 Notebook 4 takes the eight numerical labels produced in Notebook 3 and turns them into a usable business artefact: named, evidenced, marketing-ready segment profiles. This is the notebook that most directly answers the brief's request to “describe the segments you identified” and to “analyse the behaviour of the different segments”, and it does so with a consistent, repeatable template applied to all eight groups — sizes, then absolute spend, then demographic/behavioural profile, then loyalty/gender/household checks, then a normalised (z-score-style) comparison against the population average, then radar summaries, then feature-by-feature detail, then one polished “summary card” per segment, and finally a geographic sanity check.
