 Figure 13 — Inertia (within-cluster sum of squares) versus k, the classic “elbow” diagnostic. Figure 13 shows the familiar smoothly-decreasing elbow curve. As is almost always the case with real, continuously-distributed retail data, the elbow itself is gentle rather than sharp — there is no single k that screams “use me”, but the rate of improvement clearly slows somewhere in the high-single-digit range. The notebook reads this curve for what it actually offers: a plausible window for k (rather than a single answer), to be cross-checked against the sharper diagnostics that follow.
