 Two things stand out from reading the cards directly rather than relying on the segment names as a shorthand for their content. First, for several segments the headline differentiator is a *negative* deviation on promotion_% rather than a positive one on category spend — “Vegetarians”, “Wellness” and, most surprisingly, “Economizers” are all defined chiefly by buying *less* on promotion than average, which is the opposite of what their names might suggest to a reader who has not opened the cards. Second, two segments (“Promoters” and “Families”) are each defined overwhelmingly by a single, extreme outlier trait — promotion-purchase share at +145% and household size at +175% respectively — rather than by a broad constellation of smaller signals, which makes them the two easiest personas in the set to target with a single, sharply-focused campaign idea. Both observations are useful correctives to keep in mind when the executive report translates these eight cards into plain-language audience descriptions: the names are evocative shorthands chosen by the notebook's author, but the underlying “Key differentiators” bars are what should drive the actual campaign brief.
