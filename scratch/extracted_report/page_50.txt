Association Rules and Campaign Recommendations — Notebook 5 Notebook 5 closes the project loop by mining the customer_basket transactions for association rules — separately, per segment — and turning the strongest rules into concrete campaign suggestions ('if a customer in segment X buys A, offer them a promotion on B'). This is the part of the project that most directly answers the brief's request for “creative and feasible campaigns based on the segments and the rules you found”, and structurally it mirrors the discipline of the clustering notebook: the basket data is first joined back to the segment labels via customer_id, the Apriori algorithm is then run independently within each of the eight segments (rather than once on the whole population), and the resulting rules are filtered and ranked by confidence and lift before being shortlisted into the 24-row segment_campaign_rules.csv export. 8.1 Why mine rules per segment rather than globally Running Apriori once on the whole basket would surface only the strongest population-wide co-purchase patterns — typically generic, high-frequency staples that say little about any one group of customers. Mining within each segment instead allows the same algorithm to surface the patterns that are *distinctive to that audience*, even when those patterns would be statistically invisible at the whole-population scale. This is the right design choice for the stated goal (“campaigns based on the segments”): a rule that only shows up inside the Promoters segment, for example, is far more useful for designing a Promoters-specific campaign than a rule that is true of everyone and therefore differentiates nobody. 8.2 Train/test robustness checks Before trusting any rule enough to build a campaign recommendation around it, the notebook checks whether the mined rules hold up on a held-out slice of the basket data — i.e., it does not simply report whatever Apriori returns on the full sample, but verifies that the same antecedent → consequent pairs keep showing similar confidence and lift when the data is split. This guards against the well-known risk with association-rule mining on sampled transactional data (only 100,000 baskets are available, spread thinly across eight segments and thousands of possible item combinations): with this little data per segment, it would be easy for Apriori to surface a rule that is really just sampling noise dressed up as a 0.30-confidence pattern.
