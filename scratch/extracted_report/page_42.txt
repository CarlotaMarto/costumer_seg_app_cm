Figures 39a–b repackage the same normalised-deviation information from Figure 38 into radar charts — a presentation format that is far easier for a non-technical stakeholder (e.g., a marketing manager reading the executive report) to absorb at a glance than a table of z-scores. Each segment's “shape” on the radar is effectively its fingerprint: a segment whose radar bulges out on alcohol and video games tells a very different story from one that bulges out on hygiene and wellness-adjacent categories, and the visual contrast between two radar shapes is a quick, intuitive way to confirm that two segments are genuinely distinguishable rather than near-duplicates of each other. 7.7 Feature-by-feature detail plots
