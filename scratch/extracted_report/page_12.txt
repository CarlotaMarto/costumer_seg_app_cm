 Figure 8 — Overlap between the three outlier-detection methods, showing how many customers were flagged by one, two, or all three methods simultaneously. This is one of the more thoughtful decisions in the whole project. Rather than picking one outlier rule (say, a simple 1.5×IQR cut) and removing whatever it flags, the notebook runs three independent detection methods and only separates a customer into the outlier subset when all three agree (Figures 7 and 8 show, respectively, how many customers each method flags on its own and how much the three flagged sets overlap). This “consensus” approach is conservative by design — it protects genuinely high-value customers from being discarded just because one heuristic is too aggressive, while still pulling out the small set of truly atypical records (1,023 customers, about 3.1% of the base) whose extreme values would otherwise distort the cluster centroids.
