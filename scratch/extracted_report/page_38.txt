Figure 34 is the first and arguably most important quality check on the whole segmentation: are the eight groups usable audiences, or is the partition dominated by one giant “everyone else” cluster and a handful of slivers? The chart shows a healthy, fairly even spread — every segment falls roughly in the 9%–20% range of the 33,038-customer base, with no group small enough to be dismissed as noise and no group so large that it would function as a default catch-all. This balance is a direct, visible payoff of the careful k-selection work documented in Section 6.5: a less disciplined choice of k (or a less careful feature set) very often produces one mega-cluster and several tiny ones, which would have made the segmentation far less actionable for a marketing team. 7.2 Why absolute (not just relative) spend still matters
